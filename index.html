Elderly
and food...

Seniors tend to lose balance in their diet by consuming unhealthy meals, outside and inside the home, or even skipping them. This leads to an increased risk and propensity for the onset of new diseases. The physiological, psychological and social changes during aging affect elderly's dietary choices. Many older adults have inadequate energy and protein intake.

How
might we...

How might we encourage healthy eating for elderly people living alone with fair or poor physical or mental health, in order to achieve better health and prevent the onset of new diseases?

About CLOD

CLOD is a modular system with adjustable temperatures, which helps elderly to manage food, reduce waste and improve their nutrition.

The goal is to help users keep track of expiring dates while motivating them to cook by suggesting recipes everyday.

A tablet will guide the user by giving them an overview of the food that they have at home, as well as suggesting the best temperature to store the food.
SENSORS

CLOD uses a bar code reader to scan the food’s bar code and keep track of expiring dates.

SENSORS

In the module you can find temperature and humidity sensors to always grant the best environment for the food.

SENSORS

Proximity sensors are used to make sure the door is closed so that there is no temperature dispersion

SENSORS

Weight sensors are used to keep track of the food inside the module

CONNECTION

The three components of CLOD (module, barcode reader, tablet) communicate through the zigbee connection.

CONNECTION
The information stored locally is also connected to some databases in the cloud connected through wifi

Disclaimer

When dealing with a fragile audience who live alone, it’s fundamental to think about the different aspects of a very compromising issue which is food management.
The goal of this project has always been to accompany elderly in their lives, without restraining them: we believe in giving freedom to our users, therefore CLOD is a tool to assist them, and not to control them.

Having a healthy diet is clearly very important to prevent health issues, but CLOD doesn’t solely focus on the health of the body: presenting stimulous information such as recipes, and giving constant feedback about the food helps elderly feel in control, stimulated and powerful in managing their own lives. Therefore we can say that the direction of this project has always been towards cognitive incitation, resulting in a use of technology that doesn’t overtake or track the lives of elderly, but that allows them to have a true companion day to day.
